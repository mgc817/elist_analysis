# Company Background
NexTech is a US-based e-commerce company specializing in consumer electronics and accessories sold through its website and mobile app. Founded in 2018, the company has grown to serve a global customer base of 87,628 customers across 194 countries.

This analysis examines NexTech's commercial performance from 2019–2022, focusing on sales trends, product performance, and evaluation of the loyalty program and marketing channels, aiming to deliver data-driven recommendations to improve profitability and customer satisfaction.

-  Demand follows a predictable Sep–Jan seasonal pattern that remains under- optimized.
-  The loyalty program now represents ~55% of revenue share, yet members show lower retention (16%) and purchase frequency (1.18 orders) than non-members —            signaling an engagement gap worth addressing. Overall repeat purchase rate sits at ~20%.

## Sales Trends
<p align="center">

**Monthly average: $574K revenue | 2,206 orders | $250 AOV | 2.39% MoM growth rate**
-  Revenue peaked in 2020 at $10.1M (+163% YoY) with the best month being Dec 2020 ($1.2M, 4K orders), fueled by elevated demand for high-ticket laptops, with orders growing +101% () and AOV rising +31% ($253). The sharp -49% revenue drop (Dec 2020 → Feb 2021) suggests this demand was largely seasonal or one-time (likely due to increased remote work during COVID).
- Decline set in through 2021 and accelerated into 2022. In 2021, revenue fell to $9.1M (-10.14% YoY) despite a slight increase in order count (+6%, 35,858 orders), as high-AOV laptop purchases softened. 2022 saw a sharper contraction to $4.9M (-46% YoY), hitting a lifetime low in Oct 2022 ($178K, 825 orders) — likely reflecting post-pandemic demand normalization and growing retention gaps. 
-  This deceleration is visible in MoM growth trends: the 2020 monthly average of +11.14% fell to just +0.01% in 2021 and -5.45% in 2022. Notably, 2022 outperformed 2019 in Q1–Q3, but Q4 2022 marked the company's worst quarterly performance to date — underscoring NexTech's dependence on a strong holiday season.

**Seasonality:** Revenue and orders reliably peak Sep–Jan (back-to-school + holiday period), with steep declines in February (-30% MoM) and October (-18–24% MoM). These windows represent clear opportunities for targeted promotions to smooth revenue valleys.

## Product Performance

Top Performers:
Four products generate ~96% of total revenue ($27M+ of $28M):

- 27" 4K Gaming Monitor — $9.8M, 23.4K orders
-  Apple AirPods — $7.7M, 48.4K orders (most ordered product)
-  MacBook Laptop — $6.2M, AOV $1,588
-  ThinkPad Laptop — $3.2M, AOV $1,100
Apple products alone account for 51% of total revenue ($14.2M) and dominate order volume, reflecting strong brand-driven demand across the catalog.

Underperformers:
The Bose Soundsport Headphones are the weakest product in the catalog: only 27 orders and $3,339 in total revenue from 2019–2022, despite a $124 AOV that positions them as a budget alternative to the AirPods. The lack of traction suggests either poor visibility or weak product-market fit within NexTech's customer base. (Why does it underperform? - may omitt) 

AOV & Pricing Landscape
The MacBook ($1,588), ThinkPad ($1,100), and iPhone ($741) carry the highest AOVs — and the two Apple products among these also exhibit the most volatile AOV trends over time. The AirPods sit at a mid-range $160 AOV but lead in volume, making them NexTech's highest-revenue product by orders. At the low end, the Samsung Charging Cable ($20 AOV) still drives ~21K orders (~20% of total order share) — a standout considering its price point.

Growth Patterns
Laptop sales surged in 2020 (+220–400% YoY) due to remote work during COVID before declining sharply in following years — mirroring the overall company revenue arc and underlining NexTech's exposure to external demand shocks. The iPhone, despite a $741 AOV, accounts for less than 1% of total revenue, representing meaningful untapped potential if prioritized.

Expansion Opportunities
The accessory segment shows strong latent demand. The Samsung Charging Cable's ~21K orders at only $20 AOV demonstrate that customers are already buying accessories at scale. Expanding Apple accessory offerings (e.g., Apple charging cables, cases) would capitalize on existing Apple brand loyalty and likely benefit from higher AOVs than comparable non-Apple accessories.

# Regional Trends

Revenue is geographically concentrated. North America drives ~52% of total revenue ($14.5M), led by the U.S. alone at $13M. Just four countries — U.S., Canada, UK, and Japan — account for ~63% of total sales.

- All regions followed the same pandemic boom-and-normalization arc. AOV spiked across the board in early 2020 (e.g., U.S. +26%) before declining through 2021–2022, suggesting the revenue surge was a global demand event rather than any region-specific trend.
- Japan is a high-value but high-volatility market. It carries the highest AOV of any region (~$363, peaking at $614) driven largely by MacBook demand (+392% YoY in 2020) — but declined sharply post-peak, indicating sensitivity to premium product cycles rather than stable underlying demand. Marketing the Apple iPhone and other accessories here could be profitable given the popularity of Apple brand already present.

**Underutilized Channels:** Social Media and Affiliate channels declined steadily since 2019 (SM: 1.5% → <1%, Affiliate: 4.85% → 2.75%), indicating missed opportunities in scalable acquisition channels.
- Email as a Key Driver (but volatile): Email grew from ~7% of sales (2019) to peaks of 17–18% (Dec 2021–2022), with strongest performance during seasonal periods. The post-2021 decline suggests diminishing returns without further optimization — particularly in segmentation and targeted promotions.

**Recommendations:** Double down on email with better segmentation, timing, and promotional cadence — and extend what's working for Gaming Monitors to Apple Products (Macbook Air, iphone). Simultaneously, re-evaluate Social and Affiliate investment before these channels erode further.
# Loyalty Program Evaluation
<p align="center">
<img width="594" height="283" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/0496f859-7f5a-471f-818e-2f84370e2a7e" />

- Non-members still drive the majority of total revenue ($17M vs. $11M), but their share has declined steadily from 89% (2019) to 45% (2022) — while member share has grown from 11% (2019) to 55% (2022).
- While on average Members spend less per order ($232 vs. $263), Member AOV has grown +6% YoY vs. +2% for non-members, and members actually outspent non-members by $30 in 2022 — a positive trend worth monitoring.
- Non-members retain and reorder at nearly double the rate: Returning customer rate is 23% for non-members vs. 16% for members, and non-members average 2.28 orders vs. 1.18 for members — the loyalty program's biggest underperformance area.
- Membership grew to 16K in 2021 then dropped ~40% to 9.9K in 2022. This churn warrants direct investigation — nearly half of members left in a single year with no clear identified cause in the current data.
- **MacBook is a significant blind spot for members:** Despite being NexTech's highest-revenue product, MacBook purchases by loyalty members have been negligible across all years — representing an untapped cross-sell opportunity within an already engaged customer base.
# Refund Rates
<p align=center>
  <img width="350" height="110" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/f6dc7709-f091-4f8c-950b-65360241fd2a" />

Refunds represent a meaningful revenue leakage concentrated in peak periods: NexTech recorded a 5.2% refund rate (~$2.2M refunded), with the majority occurring in 2020, indicating quality or expectation gaps during high-demand periods.

- **High-value products drive disproportionate refund risk:** MacBooks and ThinkPads (~11–12% refund rates) have the highest return rates and largest refund amounts, suggesting potential issues with product expectations, pricing sensitivity, or customer fit.
- **High-volume products contribute most to refund volume:** AirPods (2.6K refunds) and Gaming Monitors (1.4K) account for the majority of returns, highlighting operational impact driven by scale rather than defect rate.
- **Apple products dominate refund impact across both volume and value:** Apple products account for ~58% of all refunds (~$1.2M), with refunds heavily concentrated in 2020 and declining afterward—indicating both brand reliance and potential improvement post-peak.
