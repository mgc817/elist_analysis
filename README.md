# Company Background
NexTech is a US-based e-commerce company specializing in consumer electronics and accessories sold through its website and mobile app. Founded in 2018, the company has grown to serve a global customer base of 87,628 customers across 194 countries.

This analysis examines NexTech's commercial performance from 2019–2022, focusing on sales trends, product performance, and evaluation of the loyalty program and marketing channels, aiming to deliver data-driven recommendations to improve profitability and customer satisfaction.
# Executive Summary
<img width="372" height="240" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3b757801-9278-4920-8d82-668a37ba6a2d" />
* NexTech generated $28M across 108K orders from 2019–2022, with growth peaking in 2020 at $10M (+163% YoY), largely due to demand for high-ticket laptops. In particular, the Macbook Air Laptop accounted for $2.9M in revenue in 2020, +384% increase from 2019. Similarly, the Lenovo Thinkpad produced $1.3M, a 222% increase from the previous year. This was followed by a sustained post-pandemic decline, as total sales dropped -10% in 2021 and another -46% in 2022.
* Revenue is highly concentrated: the top four products (27in 4K Gaming Monitor, Apple AirPods, MacBook Air, Lenovo ThinkPad) drive ~96% of sales, with the Apple brand alone contributing ~51% of total revenue — and ~58% of $2.2M in refunds. Demand follows a predictable Sep–Jan seasonal pattern that remains under-optimized.
* The loyalty program now represents ~55% of revenue share, yet members show lower retention (16%) and purchase frequency (1.18 orders) than non-members — signaling an engagement gap worth addressing. Overall repeat purchase rate sits at ~20%.
