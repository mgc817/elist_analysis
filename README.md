# Company Background
NexTech is a US-based e-commerce company specializing in consumer electronics and accessories sold through its website and mobile app. Founded in 2018, the company has grown to serve a global customer base of 87,628 customers across 194 countries.

This analysis examines NexTech's commercial performance from 2019–2022, focusing on sales trends, product performance, and evaluation of the loyalty program and marketing channels, aiming to deliver data-driven recommendations to improve profitability and customer satisfaction.

From 2019-2022, NexTech generated $28M across 108K orders, with growth peaking in 2020 at $10M (+163% YoY) largely due to heightened demand for high-ticket laptops.
-  Macbook Air Laptop accounted for $2.9M in revenue in 2020, +384% increase from 2019. Similarly, the Lenovo Thinkpad produced $1.3M, a 222% increase from the        previous year.
This was followed by a sustained post-pandemic decline, as total sales dropped -10% in 2021 and another -46% in 2022.
-  Revenue is highly concentrated: the top four products (27in 4K Gaming Monitor, Apple AirPods, MacBook Air, Lenovo ThinkPad) drive ~96% of sales, with the Apple brand alone contributing ~51% of total revenue — and ~58% of $2.2M in refunds.
-  Demand follows a predictable Sep–Jan seasonal pattern that remains under- optimized.
-  The loyalty program now represents ~55% of revenue share, yet members show lower retention (16%) and purchase frequency (1.18 orders) than non-members —            signaling an engagement gap worth addressing. Overall repeat purchase rate sits at ~20%.

## Sales Trends

**Monthly average: $574K revenue | 2,206 orders | $250 AOV | 2.39% MoM growth rate**
-  Revenue peaked in 2020 at $10.1M (+163% YoY) with the best month being Dec 2020 ($1.2M, 4K orders), fueled by elevated demand for high-ticket laptops, with orders growing +101% () and AOV rising +31% ($253). The sharp -49% revenue drop (Dec 2020 → Feb 2021) suggests this demand was largely seasonal or one-time (likely due to increased remote work during COVID).
- Decline set in through 2021 and accelerated into 2022. In 2021, revenue fell to $9.1M (-10.14% YoY) despite a slight increase in order count (+6%, 35,858 orders), as high-AOV laptop purchases softened. 2022 saw a sharper contraction to $4.9M (-46% YoY), hitting a lifetime low in Oct 2022 ($178K, 825 orders) — likely reflecting post-pandemic demand normalization and growing retention gaps. 
-  This deceleration is visible in MoM growth trends: the 2020 monthly average of +11.14% fell to just +0.01% in 2021 and -5.45% in 2022. Notably, 2022 outperformed 2019 in Q1–Q3, but Q4 2022 marked the company's worst quarterly performance to date — underscoring NexTech's dependence on a strong holiday season.

**Seasonality:** Revenue and orders reliably peak Sep–Jan (back-to-school + holiday period), with steep declines in February (-30% MoM) and October (-18–24% MoM). These windows represent clear opportunities for targeted promotions to smooth revenue valleys.
